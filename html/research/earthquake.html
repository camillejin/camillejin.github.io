Developing Earthquake VR for Design Research

Description

During the summer of 2022, I interned at the Digital Media Lab, Yonsei University.
I took on the role of developing an earthquake simulation VR,
which will be used for experiment in a design research.
The research aimed to test the different designs of mobile application that were
developed in order to help people with evacuation when earthquake occurs.
The program was required to observe the subjects' behavior in a simulated earthquake.
(This research was a national research project.)

Process

Prior to the actual development, I collected visual data on how the earthquake should appear.
I did a brief research on the effects of earthquakes according to the intensities
and collected video clips for reference. This helped in making sure that the lead researchers
and I were on the same page. After selecting the intensity level of the earthquke simulation program,
based on the reference clips, I made a list of Unity assets that would be required for the program.
The assets were then implemented onto the program to simulate earthquake.
I wrote C# scripts, created timeline, and tested with Oculus Quest 2 for fine-tuning.
During the process, So Yeon Kim and other Graduate students at the lab provided excellent advice and support.
