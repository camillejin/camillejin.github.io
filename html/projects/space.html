Spacepick

Group Project
Timeline: Sep 2021 - Dec 2021

“Spacepick" is a mobile app developed with UX design methodologies.

Motivation
Seoul, South Korea provides a number of examples for how rapid urbanization and gentrification leads to negative impact on a local area. Areas going through gentrification report a decrease in the average duration of business as higher cost makes it difficult for the original business to continue. "Spacepick" attempted to address the issue of small local businesses quickly disappearing in the gentrifying neighborhoods. The idea was to create a social networking service that focuses on locations so that the platform serves as a marketing platform for local business and therefore achieve sustainability.

Interview & Survey
Four people in their twenties were interviewed in-depth to find out how they decide where to spend time. The interviewees commonly answered that they kept their own list of places they like. They also have recommended a location to their friends, and have received recommendations from their friends. After these interviews, we wanted to see if the mentioned points were true for larger group of people. Thus, we conducted a survey on how people share information about locations. According to the survey, more than half the respondents answered that they have a list of places/shops they like, and more than 75% responded that they have experience of recommending locations to others.

Analysis and Affinity Diagram
We also used affinity diagram to organize the information acquired from the survey and to brainstorm ideas about our service. Noticing that a lot of users have a location that they prefer than the others, we deduced that preferences or thoughts on locations could be part of one's identity. Potentially, this could be used to motivate people to keep more record of the locations they visited.

Persona and Scenario
Then we created two hypothetical characters to see how our service would be utilized by those characters. The personas' needs and frustration were drawn from the survey response, and in the scenario, I attempted to figure out how those issues may be addressed with our service.

User Journey Map, Needs Analysis, HMW Exercise
Furthermore, user journey map, needs anlaysis, and hmw exercise were conducted to design the detailed function of our service.

Flowchart and Information Architecture
After we had a more clear idea about our service, we worked on the detailed flowchart and information architecture to see what visual elements are needed on the application.
